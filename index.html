About Me

Welcome! I am a PhD student in Statistics at Stanford University. I am broadly interested in problems in Causal Inference and Machine Learning, with applications to business analytics or social media. I completed my undergraduate degree in 2012 at Harvard University, and hold a Master of Science in Mathematics and Statistics from Georgetown.

Prior to Stanford, I worked at Applied Predictive Technologies from 2012 to 2015 as a Product Manager. I concurrently enrolled and completed my Master's degree at Georgetown as a night student. At Harvard, I studied Applied Mathematics, with a focus field of Physics. I also pursued a secondary field in English and completed the course requirements for an additional secondary field in Computer Science.

In my college extracurricular life, I directed 2011 science content for The Harvard Crimson as Science News Executive. Links to my articles can be found on the Articles page. A full list of my qualifications and experience can be found on the Experience page.

In addition to my work in student journalism, I am also interested in poetry. A sample of some of my work can be found on the Poetry page.
